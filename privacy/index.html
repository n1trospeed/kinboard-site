Privacy policy

Effective 4 July 2026

kinboard is a shared note board for households, built so that we — the people who run it — can read as little as technically possible. This page says plainly what we can and cannot see.

What we cannot see

Everything your family writes — notes, page titles, member names — is end-to-end encrypted on your devices. The encryption keys are created on your devices and never leave them. Our server stores and forwards only ciphertext: we cannot read your board, and neither can anyone who obtains our server's data.

What our server does handle

  • Encrypted data blobs (unreadable to us), stored so your devices can sync.
  • Random identifiers for boards and devices, and message sizes and timestamps — needed to route and order sync traffic.
  • Ordinary connection logs (IP addresses), kept briefly for operating the service.

Our sync server currently runs on Amazon Web Services in Singapore.

Kin, the optional assistant

Kin is an assistant your family can choose to invite to a board, the same way you'd invite a person. If — and only if — you invite Kin, the pages on that board become readable by the Kin service so it can write its suggestions. To generate suggestions, that content may be processed by our AI provider (Anthropic). It is used solely to produce suggestions for your board — never for advertising, profiling, or training unrelated systems. Boards that never invite Kin remain fully end-to-end encrypted, with no exceptions.

What we don't do

  • No advertising, and no advertising SDKs.
  • No analytics or tracking in the app.
  • No selling or sharing of data with third parties.

Your data, your exit

Your notes live on your devices; deleting the app removes them from that device. To have a board's encrypted server data deleted, contact us and we'll remove it. You can remove Kin from a board at any time to stop new suggestions.

Children

kinboard is intended for adults managing a household and is not directed at children under 13.
